by Draco
Your idea inspired me a bit... how about this?

Being able to equip "Runes/Emblems/Relics" that grant a special ability for the whole time you have it equipped, with chances for them to break.

Black Onyx could give +2 HP Regen, and a 'Frigid Relic' could give +5% Ice Magic Damage bonus.

All of these bonuses could range from...

+X HP/MP Regens

+X% HP/MP Base

+X% Damage/Magic Damage

+X% Attack/Cast Speed

+X% Bonus EXP

+X To Hit/Defense


And quite a bit more.

Each time the player takes 25% of their total HP in damage, the chance for the "relic" to break is activated... meaning if someone dies in one hit from 100% HP, there's 4 chances the relic will break.

A death would roll a chance to break with an increased 15x chance to break... so a 1% chance to break on a roll would increase to 15% chance upon a death.

With these two combined on a 1% chance to break, being killed in one hit from full HP would be a 19% chance to break right then. A 0.4% chance would be a 7.6% chance to break.

Once a relic is broken it'll simply disappear from your equipment, but an animation could be shown in the corner of your screen or something signaling your relic broke.

Only one relic can be equipped at a time.

For the sake of allowing everyone the benefit of relics, low level common use relics could be used that give like +10 HP and +10 MP, +10% Exp for players under lv 8 (high chance of breaking), ect.

What do you think?

Posted: Mon Oct 13, 2008 12:41 pm
by Draco
Slapping gems into equipment pieces is something that's been done repeatedly time and time again... with stacked effects of the same thing... in my opinion it's overused and dulled.

Allowing players to carry gems like this on them, to have effects and if one breaks, just equipping another one is a simple way players can have special effects on their characters without having to go through the whole socketing ur equipment, slapping a gem in, or whatever type of system other games have tried to get away with this... this would be as simple as equipping an LSA or a bag from xen... which a lot of people didn't have a big problem with.

Why I chose breaking rather than recharging permanent:

If it breaks, just equip another one... granted you didn't die and drop it, then someone took them; it's Simple.
I'd carry a few of 2 or 3 different gems on me, depending on what I was doing and if my current one broke.

Allowing them to break would allow them to be in the game in good quantities... rather than making them really rare and rechargable, forcing only the richer players to own them, and not letting the player easily switch between them.

If you want professions... relics could be made and sold to give other players benefits, sure.

Making them break would also add to the "crap!!!" factor Xen used to have; if you end up in a really dangerous area, it's scary to think that if you DO happen to get out alive, your rarer prized relic might break if you take too much damage that fast, unless you can unequip it in time.

In the Xen system (as an example) it's kind of hard to degrade +1 HP Regen on a relic if you've been using it for a while... unless you just have it a reduced chance it will actually regenerate during that second... or something...

Worrying about constantly keeping your gems or relics recharged and full potential in all ways possible just adds stress to the game... it's the main reason why I don't like durability or World of Warcraft at all.
